Someone <a data-transition='none'  data-transition='none'  data-ajax='false'   name="noreveal" href='topicinfo.html?id=353'>chokes</a> on an ice cube or gets <a data-transition='none'  data-transition='none'  data-ajax='false'   name="noreveal" href='topicinfo.html?id=189'>stung</a> by a bee. It is important to know when to call 9-1-1 -- it is for life-threatening emergencies.  While waiting for <a data-transition='none'  data-transition='none'  data-ajax='false'   name="noreveal" href='topicinfo.html?id=526'>help</a> to arrive, you may be able to save someone's life. Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (<a data-transition='none'  data-transition='none'  data-ajax='false'   name="noreveal" href='topicinfo.html?id=304'>CPR</a>) is for people whose hearts or breathing has stopped and the Heimlich maneuver is for people who are choking.  CPR should only be done if you have had the training. </p>
<p>You can also learn to handle common <a data-transition='none'  data-transition='none'  data-ajax='false'   name="noreveal" href='topicinfo.html?id=17'>injuries</a> and <a data-transition='none'  data-transition='none'  data-ajax='false'   name="noreveal" href='topicinfo.html?id=719'>wounds</a>. Cuts and scrapes, for example, should be rinsed with cool water. To stop bleeding, apply firm but gentle pressure, using gauze. If blood soaks through, add more gauze, keeping the first layer in place. Continue to apply pressure. </p>
